03 · How it actually works

Voice + camera → small brain → action — repeated five times per second

Same shape every tick. The robot's "loop" is just these four steps in a row, over and over.
🎤
Step 1 · Ears
You say something out loud.
A small program writes down your words ("turn left and pick up the cup").
📷
Step 2 · Eyes
The camera takes a picture, twice a second.
A different program looks at it and writes "I see a red cup, a chair, and a person."
🧠
Step 3 · Brain
The brain reads both notes and decides one move.
"Turn 30° left, then walk forward 0.5 m." It also picks the next thing to say back.
🦾
Step 4 · Body
The robot executes the move and speaks the line.
Then we go back to step 1 — the loop never stops until we say "done".

The whole loop has to happen in under 2 seconds, otherwise it feels laggy. That's why we run the brain on the powerful machines NVIDIA gives us, not on a laptop. Every box you'll hear us say tomorrow — vLLM, Nemotron, Ollama — is a different way of running that brain.

04 · Vocabulary · Part 1 of 2

The brains — the AI models that "think"

When Timur says "the LLM is slow" or "swap the VLM" — these are the things he's talking about.
TermWhat it actually means
LLM"el-el-em" · Large Language Model A program that reads and writes text. ChatGPT is an LLM. We use one to decide "what should the robot do next?" The bigger it is, the smarter — but also the slower.Like a very fast assistant who's read the entire internet but only sees text.
VLM"vee-el-em" · Vision-Language Model An LLM that also sees pictures. We give it the camera frame and ask: "describe what's in front of you." It writes back text that the brain (the LLM) then uses to decide.Same assistant, but now also wearing glasses.
Nemotron 3 Nano Omni"NEH-mo-tron Omni" The specific brain NVIDIA pre-loaded on the hackathon machines for us. It's special because it does both jobs (LLM + VLM) at once. One program sees and thinks.A two-in-one assistant. Less wiring for us.
Qwen 3.6 35B A3B"kwen thirty-five bee" The backup brain on those same machines. Made by Alibaba. Only does text (no eyes). We'd use it if Nemotron's vision doesn't work well.A blind genius. Smart, but needs us to describe the room.

05 · Your roles

Without your laptops, you are the difference between winning and losing

The build runs on Timur's laptop only. The robot only listens to one machine anyway. Your jobs are higher-leverage.
⌨️ Listener-typist
During the 30-min intro, type what the speaker says into docs/DAY_OF_BRIEF.md on Timur's laptop. Don't worry about structure — write whatever you hear. Claude turns your notes into config edits in 5 min.
🎬 Demo lead
From T+1:30 onward: record the demo on your phone (backup video), check the dashboard renders, narrate the 60-second judged run. The dashboard is our biggest visible feature — keep it on screen.
⏱️ Time keeper
We live and die by the clock. Call out loudly: "T+1:00", "T+1:30", "T+1:45 — freeze!". If the room is loud, walk over and tap shoulders. This is non-negotiable.
✂️ Cut-list enforcer
At T+1:30 if we're behind, someone must say "we drop X". Timur is too deep in the code to see it. The cuts are pre-decided (next slide). You just call them.
👁️ Quality eyes
Watch the robot move. If it's about to crash into something, hit the kill switch. If it speaks gibberish or freezes, tell us. Our heads are in code — yours are on the floor.
☕ Outside ops
Coffee runs, asking organizers questions, making sure we eat at 12:30. Sounds small, doesn't matter… until your blood sugar tanks at T+1:45.

07 · Plan B (and C, D, E)

The cut-list — we drop things on purpose, in this exact order

Cutting is not failure. Cutting is the plan. The team who ships a smaller working demo beats the team who ships nothing.
T+1:00
Cut #1. If the microphone keeps missing your voice or the speech-to-text is wrong half the time…
…we drop the microphone. We type commands into the dashboard instead. Same robot, just no voice in.
T+1:15
Cut #2. If the robot's voice (the speaker) is laggy or makes the loop slow…
…we drop the robot's voice. We show subtitles on the dashboard instead. Robot still does everything else.
T+1:30
Cut #3. If juggling two AI computers gets brittle (network drops, sync issues)…
…we drop the second computer. Run everything on one. Slower, but rock-solid.
T+1:45
Cut #4 (last resort). If the actual robot is crashing or the adapter is flaky…
…we show a video of an earlier run + drive a fake robot live. Judges see the agent thinking; the physical robot stays parked.

Cut-list enforcer: when the time hits and the symptom matches, you say it out loud. We do it. No debate. Timur literally has a Claude Code skill (/cut-list) that applies the right cut in 30 seconds.
